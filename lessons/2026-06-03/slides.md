# Why focus on Christ?

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-50 to-yellow-50 rounded-xl text-base leading-relaxed border border-amber-200">

**Elder D. Todd Christofferson** (*The Blessing of Scripture*, April 2010 General Conference):

> "The central purpose of all scripture is to fill our souls with faith in God the Father and in His Son, Jesus Christ."

</div>

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 text-sm">

<div class="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg">

**What this means for us:**
Since Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ are perfectly united, when we learn about Christ's character and role, we are also learning about Heavenly Father.

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg">

**The goal of today's skills:**
Not just to know facts about the Old Testament — but to see and know *Jesus Christ* on every page, in every story, in every symbol.

</div>

</div>

</div>

# What is a symbol of the Savior?

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg text-base leading-relaxed">

A **symbol of the Savior** is an object, image, or title in the scriptures that reminds us of Christ's role in our lives.

The Old Testament was written centuries before Christ came to earth — yet it's packed with symbols that prepared Israel (and us) to recognize and trust Him.

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**When you find a symbol, ask yourself:**

1. *How is the Savior like this symbol?*
2. *How does this comparison help me understand how He can help me in my life?*

</div>

<div class="text-xs text-gray-500 italic text-center">
Record these two questions in your notebook so you can use them in future study.
</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Give students 30 seconds to write the two questions in their notebooks before moving on.
- Emphasize: these questions turn a symbol into personal understanding — not just trivia.
-->

layout: default

# Skill 1 — Worked example

<div class="space-y-3 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg">

**Read:** <a href="https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/jer/2?lang=eng&id=p13#p13" target="_blank">Jeremiah 2:13</a> and <a href="https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/jer/17?lang=eng&id=p13#p13" target="_blank">Jeremiah 17:13</a>

The Lord calls Himself the **"fountain of living waters."**

</div>

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-4 bg-white border border-blue-200 rounded-lg space-y-2">

**Question 1:** *How is the Savior like a fountain of living water?*

→ A fountain gives life, refreshes the thirsty, never runs dry, and is always available. Christ offers living water — the gift of eternal life and the power to satisfy our deepest spiritual needs.

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-white border border-purple-200 rounded-lg space-y-2">

**Question 2:** *How does this help me understand how He can help me?*

→ When I feel spiritually empty, tired, or lost — I can turn to the Savior. He is the source of living water. He doesn't run dry.

</div>

</div>

</div>

# Skill 2 — His Actions and Words

# Learning from what He does and says

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-5 bg-green-50 rounded-lg text-base leading-relaxed">

The Old Testament records moments when the Lord speaks, commands, promises, and acts. Every one of those moments teaches us something about *who He is* and *how He helps us*.

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-teal-50 rounded-lg">

**As you read, ask yourself:**

1. *What do these words or actions teach me about the Savior?*
2. *How do these words or actions help me understand how the Savior can help me in my life?*

</div>

<div class="text-xs text-gray-500 italic text-center">
Add these two questions to your notebook next to the first skill's questions.
</div>

</div>

<!--

# Skill 2 — Worked example

<div class="space-y-3 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-4 bg-green-50 rounded-lg">

**Read:** <a href="https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/dan/3?lang=eng&id=p23-p25#p23" target="_blank">Daniel 3:23–25</a>

Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego are thrown into the fiery furnace — and Nebuchadnezzar sees a fourth person walking with them, "like the Son of God."

</div>

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-4 bg-white border border-green-200 rounded-lg space-y-2">

**Question 1:** *What does this teach me about the Savior?*

→ Jesus Christ goes *into the fire* with those who suffer. He doesn't stay outside and watch — He is present in the trial.

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-white border border-teal-200 rounded-lg space-y-2">

**Question 2:** *How does this help me understand how He can help me?*

→ Whatever hard thing I'm going through, the Savior can be there with me. I am not alone in the fire.

</div>

</div>

</div>

# This week's invitation

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-50 to-yellow-50 rounded-xl text-left text-sm space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

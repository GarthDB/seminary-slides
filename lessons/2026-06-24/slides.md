# Has the Lord ever asked you to do something you felt unqualified for?

# Solomon, newly crowned — [1 Kings 1:11–13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-kgs/1?lang=eng&id=p11-p13#p11); [3:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-kgs/3?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

**How Solomon became king:**

Nathan the prophet and Bathsheba warned David that Adonijah had claimed the throne without permission. David acted immediately — Solomon was anointed king before David died.

He didn't seize power. He was placed on the throne by God's plan.

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-green-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

**Solomon's heart in chapter 3:**

> *"And Solomon loved the Lord, walking in the statutes of David his father."* (1 Kings 3:3)

He wasn't perfect — but he was oriented toward God. That posture was what made what came next possible.

</div>

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg text-sm italic">
Consider: What does it mean to "love the Lord" at the beginning of a huge responsibility?
</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Keep this quick — the context matters, but 1–2 minutes max.
- The point: Solomon didn't choose this. He was placed here. That mirrors how we often end up in callings or responsibilities.
- "Walking in the statutes of his father" — Solomon was already trying before God appeared to him.
-->

# The Lord's offer — [1 Kings 3:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-kgs/3?lang=eng&id=p5#p5)

<div class="space-y-5 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

# Solomon's humble request — [1 Kings 3:6–9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-kgs/3?lang=eng&id=p6-p9#p6)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

Solomon started by acknowledging what God had already done:

> *"Thou hast shewed unto thy servant David my father great mercy."* (v.6)

Then he admitted his own weakness:

> *"I am but a little child: I know not how to go out or come in."* (v.7)

And he named his real problem — the people he was now responsible for:

> *"Give therefore thy servant an understanding heart to judge thy people, that I may discern between good and bad."* (v.9)

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**Mark in your scriptures** any phrases in verses 6–9 that show Solomon's attitude toward himself, toward God, and toward the people he was called to serve.

</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Have students read silently and mark — give 2 minutes.
- Key phrases to surface: "I am but a little child," "that I may discern between good and bad," "thy people."
- The request wasn't "make me smart" — it was "give me what I need to serve these people."
- That's the hinge of the whole lesson: he asked to bless others, not himself.
-->

# The Lord's response — [1 Kings 3:10–14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-kgs/3?lang=eng&id=p10-p14#p10)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-50 to-yellow-50 rounded-xl text-base leading-relaxed border border-amber-200">

> *"And the speech pleased the Lord, that Solomon had asked this thing."*
> — 1 Kings 3:10

Because Solomon didn't ask for riches or long life, the Lord gave him:
- A **wise and understanding heart** — *"so that there was none like thee before thee, neither after thee shall any arise like unto thee"* (v.12)
- **Riches and honour** beyond any king of his time (v.13)
- **Long life** — conditioned on walking in God's ways (v.14)

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-green-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**Principle:** When Solomon asked for what he needed to *bless others*, the Lord gave him that — plus everything he hadn't asked for.

</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Read v.12 aloud — it's sweeping praise.
- Note what triggered the outpouring: he asked outward, not inward. "Give me what I need to serve."
- The condition on long life (v.14) is worth noting — blessings can have conditions. Wisdom alone wasn't the end; faithfulness was still required.
- Transition: "Did it actually work? Let's look at one example."
-->

# Solomon uses the gift — [1 Kings 3:16–28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-kgs/3?lang=eng&id=p16-p28#p16)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-teal-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

Two women came to Solomon with one living baby and one dead one. Each claimed the living child was hers. There were no witnesses. No evidence.

Solomon's verdict: **divide the living child in two.**

The real mother immediately gave up her claim — *"Give her the living child, and in no wise slay it."* (v.26)

Solomon knew exactly what that meant. He gave her the child.

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg">

> *"And all Israel heard of the judgment … and they saw that the wisdom of God was in him."*
> — [1 Kings 3:28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-kgs/3?lang=eng&id=p28#p28)

</div>

<div class="p-3 bg-white border border-teal-200 rounded-lg italic text-xs">
See also: [1 Kings 4:29–32, 34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-kgs/4?lang=eng&id=p29-p32,p34#p29) — the fruits of Solomon's reign across all Israel

# The doctrine — the Lord increases our ability

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg text-sm italic">
Finish this sentence: <em>"Because of the Lord's help, I can …"</em>
</div>

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-50 to-indigo-50 rounded-xl text-base leading-relaxed border border-amber-200">

**The principle from today's lesson:**

As we humbly recognize our weakness and seek the Lord's help to bless others, He will increase our ability to accomplish His work.

</div>

<div class="grid grid-cols-3 gap-3 text-sm">

<div class="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg">
**Acknowledge weakness**
"I am but a little child." (1 Kings 3:7)
</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-green-50 rounded-lg">
**Ask to bless others**
"Give me what I need to serve." (1 Kings 3:9)
</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg">
**Receive the Lord's increase**
Wisdom, and more. (1 Kings 3:12–13)
</div>

</div>

</div>

# What Church leaders have said

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

**President Thomas S. Monson:**

> "When on the Lord's errand, we are entitled to divine support. The Lord will strengthen us for our assignments."

*Those who doubt their adequacy for what God has asked can trust that He supplies what is needed — not before the moment, but in it.*

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

**Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf:**

> "God will magnify the talents and abilities of those who offer willing hearts, making them perfect through Christ — without requiring flawlessness."

*The Lord doesn't wait for us to be qualified. He qualifies us as we go.*

</div>

</div>

# Elder Lawrence's question

<div class="space-y-5 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-teal-50 to-blue-50 rounded-xl text-base leading-relaxed border border-teal-200">

**Elder Larry R. Lawrence** invites us to kneel in prayer and ask:

> *"What is keeping me from progressing?"*

Then wait for the Holy Ghost to answer — and act on what comes.

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg text-sm space-y-2">

**Why this connects to Solomon:**

Solomon recognized exactly what was keeping him from serving well — *lack of wisdom to discern.* He named it honestly and asked God for it specifically.

We can do the same.

</div>

<div class="p-3 bg-white border border-blue-200 rounded-lg text-xs italic">
You don't have to share what comes. It's between you and the Lord.
</div>

</div>

# Discussion

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-purple-50 rounded-xl text-left text-base space-y-5 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

1. **Solomon said, "I am but a little child."** When have you felt like that? What happened when you brought that honest admission to the Lord?

2. **Solomon didn't ask for wisdom to benefit himself — he asked to be able to serve the people.** How does the purpose behind a request change what you're willing to ask for?

3. **The Lord gave Solomon everything he hadn't asked for, too.** How does that change what you think God wants for your life — not just your calling?

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Pick 1–2 questions if time is short. Question 2 usually generates the most interesting conversation — it's about motive, and students can feel the difference when they're honest.
- Leave ponder time before calling on anyone. "Think about it for a second before you answer."
- Share your own experience as prompted — a time you asked to be able to serve and felt the Lord increase your capacity.

# This week's invitation

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-50 to-yellow-50 rounded-xl text-left text-sm space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

**Try one of these in your personal study and life this week:**

1. **Ask Elder Lawrence's question.** Kneel and sincerely ask: *"What is keeping me from progressing?"* Write down the impression you receive. Take one step of faith in that direction.

2. **Read 1 Kings 3:5–14** and imagine God saying "Ask what I shall give thee" to you right now. What is the outward-facing thing you most need to be able to do His work? Ask for it specifically.

3. **Come prepared to share:** Next class, be ready to share one thing you noticed — about weakness, about God's power to magnify, or about what happens when we ask to bless others instead of ourselves.

</div>

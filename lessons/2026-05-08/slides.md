# A note on snakes in scripture

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-5 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg text-base">

**Symbolism in scripture is not always the same in each story.**

In the Garden of Eden, Satan appears as a snake (Genesis 3). But not every snake in scripture represents Satan.

In the ancient world, snakes often represented **power** — both good and bad. Egyptian pharaohs wore snake symbols on their headdresses. Moses's staff could become a serpent ([Exodus 7:10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ex/7?lang=eng&id=p10#p10)).

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**Why it matters:** Today's story features a serpent that symbolizes something very different from Satan. Keep an open mind as we read.

</div>

</div>

# [Numbers 21:4–5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/num/21?lang=eng&id=p4-p5#p4) — Israel complains

<div class="space-y-4">

<div class="p-6 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg">

Israel grew discouraged on the long wilderness journey and **spoke against God and Moses:**

> *"Why have ye brought us up out of Egypt to die in the wilderness? For there is no bread, neither is there any water; and our soul loatheth this light bread."*

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**Mark the verse:** What were they complaining about? What had they forgotten?

</div>

</div>

# [Numbers 21:6–7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/num/21?lang=eng&id=p6-p7#p6) — Fiery serpents

<div class="space-y-4">

<div class="p-6 bg-red-50 rounded-lg">

The Lord sent **fiery (poisonous) serpents** among the people; many were bitten and died.

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-green-50 rounded-lg">

The people came to Moses and said: **"We have sinned."** They asked him to pray that the serpents would be taken away.

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**Notice the pattern:** suffering → recognition of sin → **repentance** → asking for help.

</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- The word "fiery" in Hebrew (seraph) can mean "burning" — likely referring to the burning sensation of the venom.
- The response matters: they didn't just suffer, they recognized the spiritual cause and repented.
- This pattern is the Atonement pattern in miniature.
-->

# [Numbers 21:8–9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/num/21?lang=eng&id=p8-p9#p8) — Look and live

<div class="space-y-4">

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-teal-50 rounded-xl">

The Lord told Moses: *"Make thee a fiery serpent, and set it upon a pole."*

Moses made a **brass serpent** and put it on a pole. **Everyone who looked at it lived.**

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**Think about it:** Why might some people have refused to look? What would it take to believe something so simple could save your life?

</div>

</div>

# Option A — Small and simple things

<div class="space-y-4">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg text-sm leading-relaxed">

**President Dallin H. Oaks** reflected on the simplicity of what Israel was asked to do — *"Such a small thing for such a miraculous result!"* — and drew this parallel:

> "Consider the scripture study we've been taught to incorporate into our daily lives. Or consider the personal prayers and the kneeling family prayers… Consider attendance at seminary for youth… Though each of these practices may seem to be small and simple, over time they result in powerful spiritual uplift and growth."
> — ["Small and Simple Things,"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2018/04/small-and-simple-things?lang=eng) *Liahona*, May 2018, 90

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**In your notebook:** List 10 simple ways you can look to Christ. How could any of these bring healing?

</div>

</div>

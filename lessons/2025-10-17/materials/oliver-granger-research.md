# Oliver Granger Research Notes
## Added to October 17, 2025 Seminary Lesson

## Overview
Oliver Granger's story has been added as Part 1 of today's lesson, providing foundational principles that connect to the law of tithing in Part 2.

## Historical Sources & Citations

### Primary Scripture Reference
**Doctrine and Covenants 117:12-15** (July 1838)
- Verse 12: "His name shall be had in sacred remembrance from generation to generation, forever and ever"
- Verse 13: "His sacrifice shall be more sacred unto me than his increase"
- Source: https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/117

### Biographical Information
**Birth:** February 7, 1794, Phelps, New York
**Baptism:** 1832 by Brigham Young
**Challenge:** Partially blind from 1827 (lifelong condition)
**Death:** August 25, 1841, in Kirtland, Ohio

**Sources:**
- "Oliver Granger," Church History Biographical Database: https://history.churchofjesuschrist.org/chd/individual/oliver-granger-1794
- "Oliver Granger," Doctrine and Covenants Historical Resources: https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/history/doctrine-and-covenants-historical-resources-2025/people/bio-oliver-granger

### Church Service Record
**1833-1836:** Multiple missions to eastern United States
**1836:** Ordained high priest
**1837:** Appointed to Kirtland High Council
**1838:** Given assignment to settle First Presidency's financial affairs in Kirtland
**1839:** Appointed to preside over Church in Kirtland

**Source:** Church historical records documented in CHD and D&C Historical Resources

### Historical Context - 1838
**Timeline:**
- Early 1838: Saints forced to leave Kirtland due to apostasy and mob violence
- July 1838: D&C 117 received in Far West, Missouri
- July 8, 1838: D&C 119 (tithing) received in Far West, Missouri

**Situation in Kirtland:**
- Significant Church debts related to Kirtland Temple and Kirtland Safety Society
- Property and legal issues needed resolution
- Many former members had apostatized
- Hostile legal environment
- First Presidency legally vulnerable if affairs not settled

**Oliver's Assignment:**
- "Contend earnestly for the redemption of the First Presidency" (D&C 117:13)
- Settle debts and property claims
- Protect Church leaders from legal liability
- Extremely difficult task with limited chance of complete success

**Source:** "Doctrine and Covenants 117," Doctrine and Covenants Student Manual (2017)

## Four Seminary Themes Emphasized

### 1. Sacrifice Over Success
**Principle:** God values our faithful effort more than measurable results

**Application to Oliver:**
- He didn't fully resolve all financial problems in Kirtland
- Some debts remained, some property wasn't recovered
- Yet the Lord said "his sacrifice shall be more sacred unto me than his increase" (D&C 117:13)

**Source:** Seminary curriculum emphasis on D&C 117

### 2. Stewardship and Responsibility
**Principle:** Temporal and spiritual matters are intertwined in Church service

**Application to Oliver:**
- Given responsibility for temporal/financial matters
- Shows that spiritual service includes practical, worldly tasks
- Being a good steward means managing what the Lord entrusts to us
- Money, time, talents, resources all require faithful stewardship

**Connection to Tithing:** Tithing is about stewardship - acknowledging God owns everything

**Source:** General Handbook teaching on stewardship principles

### 3. Overcoming Adversity / Limitations
**Principle:** Faithfulness is not contingent on perfect capability

**Application to Oliver:**
- Partially blind from 1827 onward
- Travel, reading documents, and business dealings extremely difficult
- Also faced personal financial challenges
- Yet he accepted the call and did his best

**Modern Application:** Our limitations don't disqualify us from serving

**Source:** Church History Biographical Database notes on his blindness

### 4. Eternal Remembrance / Divine Recognition
**Principle:** God does not forget righteous acts, even when they're not widely known

**Application to Oliver:**
- D&C 117:12 - "his name shall be had in sacred remembrance...forever and ever"
- While Oliver isn't well-known today, the Lord remembers him
- God keeps perfect records of our service and sacrifices
- We serve for an audience of One - the Lord
- Human recognition is temporary; divine recognition is eternal

**Modern Application:** Serve faithfully even when no one else notices

**Source:** D&C 117:12-15

## Connection to Tithing Lesson

### Parallel Principles

| Oliver's Example | Our Tithing |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Gave faithfully despite challenges | Give faithfully despite financial concerns |
| Trusted the Lord's promises | Trust the Lord's promises about blessings |
| Prioritized obedience over outcomes | Prioritize obedience over financial fears |
| Received eternal recognition | Receive eternal blessings |

### Why This Introduction Works

1. **Same Historical Period:** Both D&C 117 (Oliver) and D&C 119 (tithing) were received in July 1838 during times of financial hardship

2. **Same Principles:** Both teach about sacrifice, stewardship, faith, and trusting God's promises

3. **Relatable Application:** Oliver's story makes abstract principles concrete before applying them to tithing

4. **Builds Faith:** Seeing how God honored Oliver's faithful sacrifice builds confidence to pay tithing

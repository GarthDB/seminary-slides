# What does it take to change a habit?

<div class="space-y-6 max-w-3xl mx-auto text-lg">

<div class="p-8 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-cyan-50 rounded-xl text-left">

For 27 years, UCLA basketball coach **John Wooden** started every season the same way — by having his players take off their socks, then teaching them how to put them back on properly, smoothing out every wrinkle to avoid blisters.

Wooden believed small habits could prevent setbacks and lead to success. From 1964 to 1975, his teams won **10 national championships**, including a record seven in a row.

</div>

<div class="text-sm text-gray-600 italic">
Like his players, you can build success by paying attention to small, meaningful habits.
</div>

</div>

<!--

- Transition: "This lesson can help you seek Heavenly Father's and Jesus Christ's help to develop healthy habits."

# Tearing down before building up — [Judges 6:11–16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/judg/6?lang=eng&id=p11-p16#p11)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-red-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

After a period of wickedness, the Israelites were oppressed by the Midianites. The Lord prepared a man named **Gideon** to help deliver Israel from their enemies (see [Judges 6:11–14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/judg/6?lang=eng&id=p11-p14#p11)).

As he received the Lord's assignment, Gideon expressed concern that he would not be able to fulfill it (see [Judges 6:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/judg/6?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)).

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**Read and mark:** the comforting promise the Lord made to Gideon in [Judges 6:16](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/judg/6?lang=eng&id=p16#p16).

</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Gideon doubted himself, and the Lord answered that doubt with a promise of presence, not a guarantee of ease.
- Bridge: after comforting Gideon, the Lord asked him to complete two tasks.

# Two tasks, in order — [Judges 6:25–26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/judg/6?lang=eng&id=p25-p26#p25)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-green-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

The Lord asked Gideon to **tear down** his father's altar to Baal — **then build** a new altar to the Lord in its place (see [Judges 6:25–26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/judg/6?lang=eng&id=p25-p26#p25)).

*Baal was a pagan god the people worshipped instead of the Lord (see Guide to the Scriptures, "[Baal](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/gs/baal?lang=eng)").*

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**Discuss:** Why do you think the Lord instructed Gideon to tear down the old altar *before* building the new one? Why does it matter which habits we get rid of, and which we develop?

</div>

</div>

# Amara's situation

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

Imagine **Amara**, a teenager living in Ghana. She has a younger sister, works a part-time job, and attends school.

Think of a situation in which Amara might feel stress, loneliness, or boredom. Then choose an unhealthy habit that emotion might trigger.

**Discuss:** How might recognizing what leads to an unhealthy habit help Amara begin to overcome it?

# Discussion

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-purple-50 rounded-xl text-left text-base space-y-5 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

1. **Gideon had to tear down an old altar before he could build a new one.** What's an old habit you'd need to "tear down" before a new one could really take hold?

2. **We all have triggers — stress, loneliness, boredom — that pull us toward unhealthy habits.** Why does simply recognizing a trigger make it easier to resist?

3. **"There is divine help for every one of us at any hour we feel to make a change."** What's one specific way you can invite that help this week?

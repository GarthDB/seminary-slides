### Key Definitions

**Immortality:**
- To live forever as a resurrected being
- A free gift to all through Jesus Christ's Atonement

**Eternal Life:**
- Exaltation - to become like God and live in His presence eternally as families
- Requires faith, repentance, covenants, and enduring to the end
- The highest degree of the celestial kingdom

### Learning Activities

1. **Sand Illustration**
   - Consider bringing sand and water to demonstrate the vastness of creation
   - Compare grains of sand to God's creations

2. **Memorization Activity**
   - Moses 1:39 is a Doctrinal Mastery passage
   - Use repetition, fill-in-the-blank, or partner recitation

3. **Journal Reflection**
   - Students reflect on:
     - How does Moses 1:39 change how I see myself?
     - What has God done for me?
     - How do I feel about God and Jesus Christ?
     - How can I participate in God's work?

4. **Discussion Questions**
   - Why did Moses ask "why these things are so"?
   - How does Moses 1:39 help you understand creation?
   - What difference does it make to know you're the purpose of creation?

### Main Doctrinal Points

1. **God has created worlds without number** (Moses 1:27-35)
   - The universe is vast beyond comprehension
   - God's power is infinite

2. **God's work and glory is to bring to pass our immortality and eternal life** (Moses 1:39)
   - This is the PURPOSE of all creation
   - Everything God does has this goal

3. **God actively works to help His children**
   - Sent Jesus Christ
   - Created the Earth
   - Revealed the gospel
   - Provided temples, prophets, scriptures
   - Gave the Holy Ghost
   - Restored His Church

4. **We can participate in God's work**
   - Study the gospel
   - Pray daily
   - Follow Jesus Christ
   - Make covenants
   - Serve others
   - Do temple work
   - Share the gospel

### Lesson Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students should:
1. Understand that God's purpose is to bring about our immortality and eternal life
2. Feel gratitude for what Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ do for them
3. Recognize their divine worth and potential
4. Identify ways they can participate in God's work
5. Have memorized or be working on memorizing Moses 1:39

### Time Allocation (40-minute class)

- Opening prayer & QT Time: 7-10 minutes
- Introduction (sand/scale of universe): 3-5 minutes
- Reading & discussing Moses 1:27-39: 10-15 minutes
- Personal application & journal time: 5-8 minutes
- Memorization activity: 3-5 minutes
- Closing & challenge: 3-5 minutes

### Notes for Teacher

- This is one of the most important lessons - Moses 1:39 is foundational to understanding God's nature and our purpose
- Focus on helping students feel how much they matter to God
- The universe exists FOR THEM
- Keep it personal and applicable
- Allow time for the Spirit to testify
- Journal/reflection time is crucial - don't skip it
- If showing videos, adjust time accordingly

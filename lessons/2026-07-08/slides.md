# Have you ever ignored simple advice because you were hoping for something more impressive?

<div class="space-y-6 max-w-3xl mx-auto text-lg">

<div class="p-8 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-cyan-50 rounded-xl text-left">

A doctor tells you to drink more water and go to bed earlier. A friend says "just pray about it." A leader suggests reading your scriptures for five minutes a day.

It sounds too easy to actually work.

**Why do we sometimes resist the simple answer?**

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

# Small and simple, drop by drop

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-cyan-50 to-blue-50 rounded-xl text-base leading-relaxed border border-cyan-200">

**Drip irrigation** waters plants a few drops at a time instead of one big soaking. It looks too small to matter — but the steady drips keep the soil moist exactly where the roots need it, and over time the plant thrives.

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg text-sm italic">

Reflect: What small, steady efforts are you making as a disciple of Jesus Christ right now — and what results are you seeing?

Ponder how true this is for you: *"I recognize how the small and simple things I do, or don't do, affect my life and my relationship with Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ."*

</div>

</div>

# Elisha's instruction — [2 Kings 5:2–4, 9–14](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/2-kgs/5?lang=eng&id=p2-p4,p9-p14#p2)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-green-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

A young captive Israelite girl tells her mistress — Naaman's wife — about a prophet in Israel who could heal him (vv. 2–4). Naaman travels to Elisha's house expecting a dramatic encounter.

Elisha doesn't even come to the door. He sends a simple message:

> *"Go and wash in Jordan seven times, and thy flesh shall come again to thee, and thou shalt be clean."*
> — 2 Kings 5:10

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**Look for:** what Naaman expected, how he reacted to the instruction, and who convinced him to actually try it (vv. 11–14).

</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Naaman was furious (v. 11) — he expected ceremony, a personal audience, maybe a dramatic gesture. Instead: wash in a muddy river seven times.
- His own servants talked him into obeying (v. 13) — "if the prophet had said do some great thing, wouldn't you? Why not this small thing?"
- He obeyed, and was healed completely (v. 14).
-->

# Option A — What are some small and simple invitations the Lord gives?

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

**President Dallin H. Oaks** taught:

> "Consider the scripture study we've been taught to incorporate into our daily lives. Or consider the personal prayers and the kneeling family prayers... Consider attendance at seminary... Though each of these practices may seem to be small and simple, over time they result in powerful spiritual uplift and growth... because each... invites the companionship of the Holy Ghost."
> — "Small and Simple Things," *Liahona*, May 2018

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**In your notebook:** list five or more small and simple things you could do that lead to great blessings. Choose one, search the scriptures and prophets for its promised blessings, and record or draw what you find.

</div>

<div class="p-3 bg-white border border-blue-200 rounded-lg text-xs italic">
Optional: watch ["Abide the Day in Christ"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/media/video/2011-03-0074-abide-the-day-in-christ) (2:30–4:23) — Sister Amy A. Wright's family strengthened through small and simple things.
</div>

</div>

# Option B — How can sharing a small testimony make a difference?

<div class="space-y-3 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

Ella's friend Greta isn't familiar with the restored Church. Greta's mother recently died, and Ella sees her struggling. Ella wants to share about eternal families — that Greta can be with her mom again — but she's nervous and doesn't want to offend her.

**Why might someone be nervous to share truths about the restored gospel?**

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-teal-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

# Now What? — [2 Kings 5:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/2-kgs/5?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-50 to-yellow-50 rounded-xl text-base leading-relaxed border border-amber-200">

> *"And he returned to the man of God, he and all his company, and came, and stood before him: and he said, Behold, now I know that there is no God in all the earth, but in Israel."*
> — 2 Kings 5:15

One small act of obedience changed Naaman's testimony completely.

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-green-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**Respond to one or both in your notebook:**
1. A small and simple thing you feel you're already doing well — how has obeying it strengthened your relationship with Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ?
2. A small and simple thing you feel inspired to do better — what will you do to improve, and how could that strengthen your faith?

</div>

</div>

# Discussion

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-purple-50 rounded-xl text-left text-base space-y-5 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

1. **Naaman expected something grand and got something small.** When has the Lord (or someone wiser than you) asked you to do something simpler than you expected? How did you respond at first?

2. **Naaman's own servants — not Elisha — convinced him to obey.** Who has spoken a small, ordinary word of testimony to you that ended up mattering a lot?

3. **"Obedience to the Lord's small and simple invitations brings great blessings."** What's one small and simple thing you could commit to doing consistently this week?

</div>

# This week's invitation

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-50 to-yellow-50 rounded-xl text-left text-sm space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

**Try one of these this week:**

1. **Do the small thing.** Choose one small and simple habit — prayer, scripture study, an act of service — and do it every day this week, even when it feels too small to matter.

2. **Speak a small message.** Look for one moment to share a short, honest testimony with someone the way the servant girl, the messenger, and the servants did for Naaman.

3. **Come prepared to share:** Next class, be ready to share one blessing you noticed from a small and simple act of obedience.

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Keep the invitation concrete — a specific habit, a specific person, not just "try harder."
- Remind students the Lord doesn't need grand gestures; He asks for steady, small obedience.

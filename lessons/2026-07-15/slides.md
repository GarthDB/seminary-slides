# A kingdom that forgot God — [2 Kings 21](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/2-kgs/21?lang=eng)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-red-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

Over time, the scriptures were lost, and the kingdom of Judah forgot about God. Kings **Manasseh** and **Amon** led the people to do terrible things — worshipping idols, even sacrificing children.

When Amon died, his son **Josiah** became king. He was only **eight years old**.

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**Notice:** it took two generations of bad kings to lose the scriptures entirely — but it only took one good, young king willing to seek the Lord to start finding them again.

</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Keep the description of Manasseh/Amon brief and age-appropriate — the point is the spiritual drift, not the details.
- Josiah's age (8) is worth pausing on: he inherited a spiritual mess he didn't create, and still chose to be a good king.
- After ruling 18 years, Josiah paid workers to repair the temple in Jerusalem (2 Kings 22:1–7) — set up the next slide's discovery.
-->

# The book of the law is found — [2 Kings 22:8–13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/2-kgs/22?lang=eng&id=p8-p13#p8)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-green-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

While workers repair the temple, **Hilkiah** the priest finds the "book of the law" — the scriptures — buried and forgotten inside it.

King Josiah decides to have the book read aloud to **all the people of Jerusalem** (see 2 Kings 22:14–20; 23:1–2).

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**Mark in your scriptures:** what Josiah does the moment he hears the words of the law read to him (2 Kings 22:11).

</div>

</div>

<!--

# The people covenant — [2 Kings 23:1–3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/2-kgs/23?lang=eng&id=p1-p3#p1)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-50 to-yellow-50 rounded-xl text-base leading-relaxed border border-amber-200">

Josiah reads the book of the law to all the people. Then he — and the whole nation — make a covenant before the Lord "to walk after the Lord, and to keep his commandments... with all their heart and all their soul."

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**Read and mark:** what the people did after Josiah read them the scriptures (2 Kings 23:1–3).

</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- This is the hinge of the whole story — hearing scripture leads directly to covenant-making.
- Note the phrase "with all their heart and all their soul" — total, not partial, turning.
-->

# Turning to the Lord — [2 Kings 23:3–4, 21, 24–25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/2-kgs/23?lang=eng&id=p3-p4,p21,p24-p25#p3)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

As Josiah and his people learned from the scriptures and turned to the Lord, they were inspired to make changes in their lives — destroying idols and restoring true worship and obedience to the law of Moses.

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**Mark in your scriptures:** what King Josiah did to restore true worship of the Lord.

# Option A — How can scripture study help me turn toward God?

<div class="space-y-3 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

Read **2 Kings 23:3–4, 21, 24–25** again. Ponder:

- Why do you think the people needed to destroy all their idols to turn to the Lord with all their hearts?
- What are some things you feel the Savior might want you to cast out of your life so you can better follow Him?
- How can scripture study help us remember the Savior and live His gospel?

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**In your notebook:** identify a scripture passage that has helped (or could help) you turn to Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ. Consider your seminary tags and markings, or the doctrinal mastery passages.

Share that scripture with a friend or family member, and explain how it has helped you focus on and turn to God.

</div>

</div>

<!--

# Option B — What effect can God's word have on my life?

<div class="space-y-3 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

Read **2 Kings 23:1–3** and **[Mosiah 5:1–5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/5?lang=eng&id=p1-p5#p1)**. King Benjamin, a Book of Mormon king, also gathered his people to teach them God's word.

**Compare** King Benjamin's people with King Josiah's people. Link 2 Kings 23:3 to Mosiah 5:5.

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-teal-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

President Spencer W. Kimball taught: *"If I immerse myself in the scriptures the distance narrows and the spirituality returns."* — Teachings of Presidents of the Church: Spencer W. Kimball (2006), 67

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

**In your notebook, record:**
- Similarities between King Benjamin's people and King Josiah's people
- Connections between the people's hearts and their willingness to make and keep covenants — and how that applies to you

</div>

</div>

<!--

# Now What?

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-50 to-yellow-50 rounded-xl text-base leading-relaxed border border-amber-200">

President Spencer W. Kimball shared:

*"I am convinced that each of us, at some time in our lives, must discover the scriptures for ourselves — and not just discover them once, but rediscover them again and again. ... I feel strongly that we must all of us return to the scriptures just as King Josiah did and let them work mightily within us."*

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-green-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**Respond in your notebook:**
1. Is your current scripture study goal helping you fully turn to Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ? If so, how have you seen this happen? If not, what will you adjust?
2. What meaningful experiences have you had reading the assigned Old Testament chapters? What will you do to complete the assigned readings?

</div>

</div>

# Discussion

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-purple-50 rounded-xl text-left text-base space-y-5 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

1. **Judah lost the scriptures for a generation, and lost God right along with them.** What keeps you connected to the scriptures on ordinary weeks — not just in a lesson like this one?

2. **Josiah didn't just read the book — he tore his clothes, then led the whole nation to covenant.** What's the difference between reading scripture and letting it actually change you?

3. **"We must all of us return to the scriptures just as King Josiah did."** What's one small step you could take this week to return to, or deepen, your own scripture study?

</div>

<!--

# This week's invitation

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-50 to-yellow-50 rounded-xl text-left text-sm space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

**Try one of these this week:**

1. **Return to the book.** Set (or recommit to) a specific scripture study goal — even five minutes a day counts.

2. **Share what you find.** Share the scripture you chose in Option A with a friend or family member, and tell them why it matters to you.

3. **Come prepared to share:** Next class, be ready to share your scripture study goal, or something specific you did this week to complete the assigned Old Testament readings.

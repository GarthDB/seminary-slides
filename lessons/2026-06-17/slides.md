# The cast — [1 Samuel 25:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-sam/25?lang=eng&id=p3#p3)

<div class="grid grid-cols-3 gap-4 text-sm max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-5 bg-red-50 rounded-lg">

**Nabal**

*"Churlish and evil in his doings"* (v.3)

Wealthy. Rude. Refused to share despite David's men protecting his herds for months.

His name literally means *"fool."*

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg">

**David**

Israel's anointed king-in-waiting. Led 600 soldiers in the wilderness.

When insulted, his first reaction: *arm 400 men and march.* (v.13, 21–22)

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-green-50 rounded-lg">

**Abigail**

*"A woman of good understanding, and of a beautiful countenance"* (v.3)

Nabal's wife. Wise, quick, and willing to put herself between two men headed toward disaster.

</div>

</div>

<!--

# The offense — [1 Samuel 25:5–13, 21–22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-sam/25?lang=eng&id=p5-p13,p21-p22#p5)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

**What David had done:**
- Protected Nabal's flocks and herds in the wilderness (v.7, 15–16)
- Sent messengers politely asking for food and supplies (v.5–8)
- Made a reasonable request during a feast day

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-red-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

**What Nabal said:**
- *"Who is David?… there be many servants nowadays that break away every man from his master."* (v.10–11)
- Refused everything. Sent David's men away empty.

</div>

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**David's reaction (v.13, 21–22):** Armed 400 men and marched toward Nabal's household. *"So and more also do God unto the enemies of David, if I leave of all that pertain to him by the morning light any that pisseth against the wall."*

</div>

</div>

# Abigail intercedes — [1 Samuel 25:18–20, 23–28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-sam/25?lang=eng&id=p18-p20,p23-p28#p18)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-green-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

Without telling Nabal, Abigail loaded up donkeys with food — bread, wine, sheep, grain, raisins, figs — and rode out to meet David. She bowed before him and said:

> *"Upon me, my lord, upon me let this iniquity be."* (v.24)

She took Nabal's blame on herself. She asked David to stop. She offered herself as a shield between the offender and the offended.

</div>

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-purple-50 to-blue-50 rounded-xl text-base border border-purple-200">

**Abigail is a *type of Christ.***

In the scriptures, a type of Christ is a person, object, or event that points us toward the Savior.

What did Abigail do that Jesus Christ also does?

</div>

</div>

# Sister Yee on Abigail and Christ

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-purple-50 to-teal-50 rounded-xl text-base leading-relaxed border border-purple-200">

**Sister Kristin M. Yee**, Relief Society General Presidency:

> "In this account, Abigail can be seen as a powerful type or symbol of Jesus Christ. Through His atoning sacrifice, He can release us from the sin and weight of a warring heart and provide us with the sustenance we need. Just as Abigail was willing to take Nabal's sin upon herself, so did the Savior—in an incomprehensible way—take upon Him our sins and the sins of those who have hurt or offended us."

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-teal-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

*"Abigail's bringing an abundance of food and supplies can teach us that the Savior offers to those who have been hurt and injured the sustenance and help we need to be healed and made whole."*

— "Beauty for Ashes: The Healing Path of Forgiveness," *Liahona*, Nov. 2022

</div>

</div>

<!--

- Invite a student to read the longer quote aloud.

- The Savior doesn't just forgive the sinner — He brings sustenance (healing, strength) to the one who was wronged.
-->

# David relents — [1 Samuel 25:32–35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-sam/25?lang=eng&id=p32-p35#p32)

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-6 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg text-base leading-relaxed">

When Abigail finished speaking, David stopped. He blessed God for sending her:

> *"Blessed be the Lord God of Israel, which sent thee this day to meet me: And blessed be thy advice, and blessed be thou, which hast kept me this day from coming to shed blood."* (v.32–33)

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg text-sm space-y-2">

**David recognized three things:**
1. God *sent* Abigail — this was not an accident
2. Her presence *kept* him from a sin he would have regretted
3. Forgiveness was a gift, not a defeat

</div>

</div>

<!--

- Transition: "The question is — what does this story teach us about *how* we can actually forgive?"
-->

# The doctrine — forgiveness through Christ

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg text-sm italic">
Finish this sentence: *"Because of Jesus Christ, I can …"*
</div>

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-amber-50 to-yellow-50 rounded-xl text-base leading-relaxed border border-amber-200">

**President Russell M. Nelson:**

> "Through [the Savior's] infinite Atonement, you can forgive those who have hurt you and who may never accept responsibility for their cruelty to you. … The Savior will grant you the ability to forgive anyone who has mistreated you in any way. Then their hurtful acts can no longer canker your soul."

— First Presidency Christmas Devotional, Dec. 2, 2018

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

See also: [Matt 18:21–22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/18?lang=eng&id=p21-p22#p21) · [Luke 23:34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/23?lang=eng&id=p34#p34) · [Eph 4:32](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/eph/4?lang=eng&id=p32#p32) · [D&C 64:8–11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/64?lang=eng&id=p8-p11#p8)

# Option A — What can I learn about Jesus Christ from Abigail?

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

Read [1 Samuel 25:18–19, 23–28, 31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-sam/25?lang=eng&id=p18-p19,p23-p28,p31#p18). **Mark** anything Abigail said or did that is like something Jesus Christ has said or done.

**In your notebook, make a list:**
- What did Abigail do? → What does Christ do?

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-indigo-50 rounded-lg">

Then read [1 Samuel 25:32–35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/1-sam/25?lang=eng&id=p32-p35#p32) — David's reaction when Abigail helped him forgive.

# Discussion

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-purple-50 rounded-xl text-left text-base space-y-5 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

1. **Abigail took Nabal's blame on herself.** In what ways does Jesus Christ do something similar for us — and for those who have wronged us?

2. **David's anger was justified — at least on the surface.** Have you ever felt justified anger that you later recognized was pulling you toward something destructive? What helped?

3. **President Nelson said the Savior can help you forgive people who "may never accept responsibility."** How does that change what forgiveness means?

</div>

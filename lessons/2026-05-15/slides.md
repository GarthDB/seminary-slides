# A name worth knowing

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-5 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg text-base">

**Joshua's name is the same as Jesus.**

In Hebrew, Joshua is *Yehoshua* — "the Lord saves." In Greek, that becomes *Iesous* — Jesus. Both names carry the same meaning: **God is our salvation**.

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**Why it matters:** When the Lord says "I will be with thee" to Joshua, He's making a promise that the Savior would later embody in person. Joshua leading Israel into the promised land is a *similitude* — a type pointing forward to Christ leading us home.

</div>

</div>

# [Joshua 1:1–4](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/josh/1?lang=eng&id=p1-p4#p1) — A new leader, a huge task

<div class="space-y-4">

<div class="p-6 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg">

Moses has just died. The Lord calls Joshua to lead Israel across the Jordan River and into Canaan:

> *"Moses my servant is dead; now therefore arise, go over this Jordan, thou, and all this people, unto the land which I do give to them."*

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**Think about it:** Moses was a towering figure — miracles, the Law, forty years of leadership. What would it feel like to step into his shoes? What would Joshua need most right now?

</div>

</div>

# Option A — What courage actually is

<div class="space-y-4">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg text-sm leading-relaxed">

Courage isn't the absence of fear — it's acting *despite* fear because you trust something bigger.

Read any three of these:
- [Deuteronomy 31:7–8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/deut/31?lang=eng&id=p7-p8#p7)
- [Isaiah 40:29–31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/isa/40?lang=eng&id=p29-p31#p29)
- [John 14:27](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/14?lang=eng&id=p27#p27)
- [Romans 8:31](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rom/8?lang=eng&id=p31#p31)
- [2 Timothy 1:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/2-tim/1?lang=eng&id=p7#p7)

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**In your notebook:** Name a challenge you're currently facing. What does "strong and courageous" look like for *you* in that situation? What do you know about God that makes courage possible?

</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Isaiah 40:31 — "They that wait upon the Lord shall renew their strength" — is especially powerful here.
- 2 Timothy 1:7 — "God hath not given us the spirit of fear; but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind."
- The goal isn't to feel courageous; it's to act on what you know about God even when afraid.

# Option B — Meditating on scripture

<div class="space-y-4">

<div class="p-5 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg text-sm leading-relaxed">

**Elder D. Todd Christofferson** on what it means to *study* rather than just read:

> "When I say 'study,' I mean something more than reading… You should care more about the amount of time you spend in the scriptures than about the amount you read in that time."
> — [*Ensign*, May 2004, 11](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/ensign/2004/05/when-thou-art-converted?lang=eng)

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-teal-50 rounded-lg text-sm">

**Try it now:** Choose one verse from Joshua 1:5–9. Spend 3–4 minutes with it:
1. Read it slowly — more than once
2. Ask: *What does this tell me about God? What does He want me to do or feel?*
3. Write one insight in your notebook

**Then share:** What made it easier or harder to really *slow down* with a verse?

</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- This is a skill students can take home. The manual is teaching *how* to study, not just *what* to study.
- Give genuine quiet time — 3 full minutes is longer than it feels. Resist the urge to fill the silence.
- The cross-reference to 2 Nephi 32:3 ("feast upon the words of Christ") reinforces that this kind of deep engagement is the model.
-->

# Discussion

<div class="p-8 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-teal-50 rounded-xl text-left text-base space-y-5 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

1. **God told Joshua to "be strong and courageous" three times in nine verses.** Why do you think He repeated it? What does that tell you about how God views fear?

2. **Joshua 1:8 ties success to meditating on scripture day and night.** What's the difference between reading the scriptures and really *meditating* on them? Have you ever experienced that difference?

3. **"The Lord thy God is with thee whithersoever thou goest."** Where in your life do you most need to believe that right now?

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Question 1 opens up the idea that courage is commanded, not just encouraged — which means it's a choice, not just a feeling.
- Question 3 is the application anchor. It can be shared in pairs if students are hesitant to open up in the full group.
- 1 Nephi 17:31–40 is a great cross-reference if discussion stalls: Nephi reminds his brothers of all the times God showed up for Israel.

# President Dieter F. Uchtdorf

<div class="grid grid-cols-[1fr,200px] gap-6 items-center">

<div class="space-y-4">

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-teal-50 rounded-xl">

<div class="text-sm text-gray-800 leading-relaxed">

To **sacrifice** means to **give something up in favor of something more valuable**. Anciently, God's people sacrificed the firstlings of their flocks in honor of the coming Messiah. Throughout history, faithful Saints have sacrificed personal desires, comforts, and even their lives for the Savior.

We all have things, large and small, we need to sacrifice in order to follow Jesus Christ more completely. **Our sacrifices show what we truly value.** Sacrifices are sacred and honored by the Lord.

</div>

<div class="text-xs text-gray-600 mt-3">

— President Dieter F. Uchtdorf, ["Our Heartfelt All,"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2022/04/47uchtdorf?lang=eng) Liahona, May 2022, 124

# Burnt offerings

<div class="grid grid-cols-[1fr,1fr] gap-6 items-center">

<div class="space-y-3 text-sm">

<div class="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

The **wilderness tabernacle** held the altar of burnt offering in its outer courtyard. Priests offered animals there **daily** — a constant, visible witness pointing forward to the Savior's Atonement.

</div>

<div class="p-3 bg-amber-50 rounded-lg text-xs">

Burnt offerings were **continuous** — morning and evening, every day. Sacrifice wasn't a one-time event for ancient Israel; it was woven into the rhythm of life.

</div>

</div>

# [Leviticus 1:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/lev/1?lang=eng&id=p3#p3) — "of his own voluntary will"

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-5 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg text-base leading-relaxed">

In **Leviticus 1**, the Lord instructed Israel to make **burnt offerings**. They were daily, symbolic of the Savior's Atonement, and a sign of **continuous commitment** to the Lord.

</div>

<div class="p-5 bg-green-50 rounded-lg text-base">

**Read** [Leviticus 1:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/lev/1?lang=eng&id=p3#p3). Mark the phrase: *"He shall offer it of his **own voluntary will**."*

**Discuss:** What truth about sacrifice can you learn from this phrase?

</div>

</div>

<!--
Presenter Notes:
- Sacrifice that the Lord honors is offered freely. Coerced or grudging sacrifice misses the point.
- Connect to D&C 4:2 ("with all your heart, might, mind and strength") — same idea.
-->

# After the Atonement

<div class="grid grid-cols-[1fr,1fr] gap-5 items-center">

<div class="space-y-3">

<div class="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg text-sm leading-relaxed">

After the Savior's atoning sacrifice was completed, **He adjusted the type of sacrifice** He asks His people to make (see [3 Nephi 9:19–20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/9?lang=eng&id=p19-p20#p19)).

We no longer sacrifice animals. The Lord still asks for sacrifices — but now He asks for **a broken heart and a contrite spirit**.

</div>

<div class="p-3 bg-amber-50 rounded-lg text-xs italic">

"Ye shall offer for a sacrifice unto me a **broken heart and a contrite spirit**." — [3 Nephi 9:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/9?lang=eng&id=p20#p20)

</div>

</div>

<div class="flex justify-center">
  <img src="https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/imgs/959df8a8bba511ee8135eeeeac1ed760e6380929/full/%21500%2C/0/default" alt="Animal sacrifice and the Crucifixion" class="rounded-lg shadow-md max-h-72 object-contain" />
</div>

</div>

<!--

- Once the real sacrifice was made, the symbol gave way to a different kind of offering — the offering of ourselves.

# Sister Tracy Y. Browning

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-purple-50 rounded-xl text-base leading-relaxed max-w-4xl mx-auto">

When we bring our offering to the Savior, we are being invited to **see more of Jesus Christ in our lives**, as we humbly submit our will to Him in recognition and understanding of His **perfect submission to the will of the Father**.

<div class="text-sm text-gray-600 mt-4">

— Sister Tracy Y. Browning, ["Seeing More of Jesus Christ in Our Lives,"](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2022/10/16browning?lang=eng) Liahona, Nov. 2022, 14

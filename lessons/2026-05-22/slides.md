# Knowing vs. Using

<div class="space-y-6 max-w-3xl mx-auto text-lg">

<div class="p-8 bg-gradient-to-r from-sky-50 to-indigo-50 rounded-xl text-left">

Think about something you know how to use — a sewing machine, a radio, a car. Now think of something you've seen but couldn't actually operate.

**What's the difference between knowing *what* something is and knowing *how to use* it?**

</div>

<div class="text-sm text-gray-600 italic">
The same gap exists with doctrine. Today we practice closing it.

# Activity 1 — Create scenarios

<div class="space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto text-sm">

<div class="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg leading-relaxed">

Choose **three DM passages** (other than [Joshua 24:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/josh/24?lang=eng&id=p15#p15)). For each one, write a **realistic scenario** — a situation someone your age might face where that doctrine could make a difference.

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-green-50 rounded-lg">

**Example from the manual:**

A girl is at a party and feels pressured to do something she knows the Lord doesn't approve of. She remembers [Genesis 39:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/gen/39?lang=eng&id=p9#p9) — *"How then can I do this great wickedness, and sin against God?"* — and it gives her words to say no.

</div>

<div class="p-3 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg text-xs">

Write your three scenarios in your notebook. Be specific — the more realistic the situation, the more useful the practice.

</div>

</div>

# Options A, B, C — Choose one

<div class="grid grid-cols-3 gap-3 text-xs max-w-5xl mx-auto">

<div class="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-lg space-y-2">

## A — Act in Faith

Help the young man by writing a response based on **acting in faith** — even before he has all the answers.

**What this looks like:**
- Encourage a specific prayer or action he can take *now*
- Use a DM passage that supports living the commandment despite pressure
- Show that obedience doesn't require certainty first

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-purple-50 rounded-lg space-y-2">

## B — Eternal Perspective

Help him **examine the situation** with an eternal perspective.

**What this looks like:**
- Help him see it through Heavenly Father's plan
- Use a DM passage that puts the commandment in bigger-picture context
- Address the "it's not a big deal" claim with eternal truth

</div>

<div class="p-4 bg-green-50 rounded-lg space-y-2">

## C — Divinely Appointed Sources

Help him **seek further understanding** through sources God has established.

**What this looks like:**
- Direct him to scriptures, a prophet's words, a bishop, or a parent
- Suggest a DM passage to study more deeply
- Explain why those sources are trustworthy

</div>

</div>

# Now what?

<div class="p-6 bg-gradient-to-r from-blue-50 to-purple-50 rounded-xl text-left text-sm space-y-4 max-w-4xl mx-auto">

Write your thoughts in your notebook — choose **one or more**:

1. **Which DM passage could help the young man most, and why?** What makes that scripture powerful for his specific situation?

2. **How could the principles of acquiring spiritual knowledge** (Act in Faith / Eternal Perspective / Divinely Appointed Sources) **help him choose to follow Jesus Christ?**

3. **What truths about Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ** should he hold onto? What do those truths say about the commandment he's being pressured to break?

</div>

## ‚ùì Questions and Answers

### Why was what happened in the Kirtland Temple so significant?

The Savior promised the early Saints tremendous blessings for their labor and sacrifice to construct a temple in Kirtland, Ohio. Those blessings included the keys and powers restored on April 3, 1836.

### What was significant about the prophet Elijah's return on Easter Sunday, April 3, 1836?

In our theology, Easter is associated with the ancient Israelite celebration of Passover. Both observances are rich in symbolism pertaining to the sacrifice of the Lamb of God to bring deliverance and salvation to the Lord's covenant people.

A traditional part of the annual Jewish observance of the Passover ceremony pertains to the prophesied coming of Elijah. Typically, during the Passover feast, or Seder, a place is set at the table for Elijah, and at a certain time during the meal, the door is opened for the prophet to enter.

As it happened, April 3 was the second day of the Passover season in 1836. So, remarkably, Elijah the prophet did indeed return during Passover.

"For us, then, the prophesied coming of Elijah is a sacred thread that runs through the Feast of the Passover, the salvation of the human family celebrated at Easter, and the sublime events that occurred in the Kirtland Temple on April 3, 1836."

## üìã Lesson Summary

This lesson focuses on the restoration of priesthood keys in the Kirtland Temple on April 3, 1836, when three ancient prophets appeared to Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery:

1. **Moses** - Restored the keys of the gathering of Israel (missionary work)
2. **Elias** - Committed the dispensation of the gospel of Abraham (Abrahamic covenant)
3. **Elijah** - Restored the sealing keys (temple and family work)

These keys are essential for preparing the world for the Second Coming of Jesus Christ and provide the authority for missionary work, temple ordinances, and family sealings that bless our lives today.

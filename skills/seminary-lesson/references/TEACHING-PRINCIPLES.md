# Teaching in the Savior’s Way — seminary slide checklist

Distilled from *Teaching in the Savior’s Way* (Church manual, 2022). Official source: [churchofjesuschrist.org](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teaching-in-the-saviors-way-2022?lang=eng). Full markdown extract in the repo: `teaching-in-the-saviors-way/` (repo root).

Use this when **designing or refining** `slides.md`: visible slides, discussion prompts, and **presenter notes**—especially after `create-lesson.py` runs.

---

## Five principles to keep in mind

| Principle | In practice (seminary / Slidev) |
|-----------|----------------------------------|
| **Focus on Jesus Christ** | Tie the lesson’s doctrine to Christ’s roles, titles, Atonement, and invitations to follow Him—not only facts or history. |
| **Love those you teach** | Wording that respects every student; safe, inclusive tone; avoid shaming; assume divine potential. |
| **Teach by the Spirit** | Leave room for the Spirit (pause, ponder, testimony). Presenter notes: personal spiritual prep, follow promptings in class. |
| **Teach the doctrine** | Anchor claims in **scriptures** and **words of latter-day prophets**; focus truths that build **faith in Christ** and conversion. |
| **Invite diligent learning** | Questions and activities that help learners **seek, recognize, and act**—not only listen. |

## By deck section

**Title / welcome**

- State how the day’s focus helps learners **come unto Christ** (not only “covering the reading”).
- Keep “class structure” honest for a **40-minute** block; avoid overstuffing.

**Opening / QT Time**

- QT matches the manual’s emphasis on **learner voice** and **small groups**: student brings a question; breakouts; reconvene; brief reports.
- Presenter notes: pray for learners by name when possible; create a **safe** setting where contributions are valued.

**Scripture / manual content slides**

- Prefer **scripture text and prophetic teachings** over long paraphrase alone.
- Invite learners to **find** truths in verses (what do *you* notice?) before supplying every answer.
- Use questions that build **faith** and **relevance**: “How does this help you trust the Savior?” not only trivia.

**Discussion slides**

- **Ponder time** before calling on someone: note in presenter notes to wait silently if needed.
- Mix **inspired questions** with openness: “What would you ask the Lord about this?”
- **Resist** answering every question yourself—involve the class in seeking answers in God’s word (Bednar pattern: *What can I invite them to do?*).

**Application / challenge / closing**

- End with **clear, respectful invitations** to act; connect actions to **promised blessings** where appropriate.
- **Bear testimony** in presenter notes as prompted; invite brief student testimony or feeling when appropriate—not forced.

---

## Presenter notes (quick wins)

- Remind yourself to **prepare spiritually**, not only slides (study, pray, repent, live what you teach).
- Note where to **bear testimony** and where to **invite learners** to share feelings or experiences.
- Flag flexibility: **follow spiritual promptings** about needs in the room—even if it means skipping a slide.
- For youth: see full chapter on settings/learners below (variety, pacing, participation).

---

## QT Time and small groups (explicit manual alignment)

- Small groups and **sharing what they are learning** are consistent with *Invite diligent learning* (learners internalize truth by expressing it).
- One reporter per group keeps **all voices** heard without running long—balance **diligent learning** with time.

## One-line self-check before you finish

> Does this deck help learners **know the Savior better**, **feel loved and safe**, **engage with scripture and doctrine**, and **leave with a doable invitation**—with room for the **Spirit** and **their** voices?

If yes, you’re aligned with *Teaching in the Savior’s Way*.
